Loggerhead Sea Turtle (*Caretta caretta*)

Slide 1: Species Overview & Conservation Status

Field Detail
**Scientific Name** *Caretta caretta*
**Conservation Status** Threatened (ESA); Endangered or Vulnerable (IUCN, by population segment)
**Size (Adult)** 33–48 inches (84–122 cm) length; 175–400 lbs (80–180 kg) weight
**Distinguishing Feature** Exceptionally large head and powerful jaws [Image of Loggerhead Sea Turtle head] for crushing hard-shelled prey (crabs, mollusks).
**Diet** Omnivorous: Primarily benthic (seafloor) invertebrates, including clams, crabs, sea urchins, and shrimp.

Slide 2: Habitat and Alabama Distribution

Primary Habitat

  • **Adults:** Coastal habitats, including bays, estuaries, lagoons, and shallow coastal waters. They are often found around wrecks and reefs.
  • **Juveniles:** Spend their early years in the open ocean (pelagic zone), often floating in **Sargassum** algae mats, which act as nurseries.

Location in Alabama (NGM Recovery Unit)

  • **Status:** Loggerheads are the **most frequently encountered** sea turtle and the only species that regularly nests on Alabama beaches.
  • **Counties:** Primarily along the Gulf Coast beaches of **Mobile and Baldwin** counties.
  • **Nesting Range:** From the Florida border west to Dauphin Island, with the highest density between Fort Morgan and Gulf Shores.

Slide 3: Nesting and Reproduction

Characteristic Detail
**Nesting Season** May 1 – October 31 (Alabama); Primary nesting period is June and July.
**Nesting Time** **Nocturnal Nester** (lays eggs during the night).
**Nesting Frequency** Females nest 3 to 5 times per season at $\approx 14$-day intervals, typically nesting every 2 to 3 years.
**Clutch Size** Average $\approx 110$ eggs per nest (range: 75–200).
**Incubation Period** Approx. 50–65 days. Sex is determined by sand temperature (warmer = female).

Major Threat:

Artificial beachfront lighting disorients hatchlings, causing them to crawl away from the ocean instead of toward it.

Slide 4: Tracking Data (AI Focus: Crawl Identification)

Key Data Points for Loggerhead Tracking Model

Tracking Parameter Typical Measurement Model Feature Significance
**Crawl Width** **Avg. 70–90 cm ($\approx 25$ inches)** Primary feature to distinguish from Kemp's Ridley (narrower) and Green Turtles (wider).
**Crawl Pattern** **Alternating Crawl** Flippers move alternately (left front/right rear, then right front/left rear), creating staggered, comma-shaped indentations.
**Tail Drag Mark** **Usually Absent** Unlike Green or Leatherback turtles, Loggerheads typically do not leave a continuous tail drag mark.
**False Crawl Rate** $\approx 50\%$ The female may come ashore without nesting due to obstructions or light; this must be identified by the model.
