Gopher Tortoise (*Gopherus polyphemus*)

Slide 1: Species Overview & Conservation Status

Field Detail
**Scientific Name** *Gopherus polyphemus*
**Conservation Status** Federally Threatened (Western population, including parts of Alabama); State Protected (Rest of Alabama)
**Size (Adult)** Shell length 6–15 inches (15–37 cm); Weight $\approx 15$ lbs (7 kg).
**Distinguishing Feature** Shovel-like, flattened forelimbs used for digging deep burrows.
**Ecological Role** Keystone Species. Over 360 other species rely on their burrows for shelter.

Slide 2: Habitat and Alabama Distribution

Primary Habitat: The Longleaf Pine Ecosystem

  • **Required Soil:** Dry, deep, well-drained sandy soils are essential for digging burrows.
  • **Vegetation:** Open-canopy habitats, such as longleaf pine sandhills and scrub, maintained by frequent natural or prescribed fire.
  • **Burrow Structure:** Burrows are approximately 15 feet long and up to 10 feet deep, maintaining a stable temperature year-round.

Location in Alabama

  • **Range:** Found across the Coastal Plain of the southern half of Alabama.
  • **Federally Threatened:** Applies to populations west of the Mobile and Tombigbee Rivers (Choctaw, Washington, and Mobile counties).
  • **High Density Sites:** Largest protected populations are found in Conecuh National Forest and Fort Rucker Military Base.

Slide 3: Reproduction and Life Cycle

Characteristic Detail
**Nesting Season** May to July. Females typically lay one clutch per year.
**Nesting Location** In the sandy 'apron' (mound of sand) immediately outside the burrow entrance, or nearby in a sunny, open patch.
**Clutch Size** Average $\approx 6$ eggs (range: 3–15 eggs). Western populations (like Alabama's) tend to have smaller clutches.
**Incubation Period** Approx. 80–100 days. Hatching usually occurs in September.
**Sex Determination** Temperature-Dependent: Warmer nests produce females; cooler nests produce males.

Key Vulnerability:

Hatchlings are highly vulnerable to predation (raccoons, foxes, fire ants) and human activity until their shells fully harden (which takes 6-7 years).

Slide 4: Tracking Data (AI Focus: Burrow and Track Identification)

Key Data Points for Tortoise Tracking Model

Tracking Parameter Typical Measurement Model Feature Significance
**Burrow Entrance Shape** **Half-moon / Mousehole** Flat bottom and arched top, matching the shape of the tortoise's shell.
**Burrow Width** **$\approx$ Tortoise Shell Length** Used to estimate the size (and therefore age/maturity) of the tortoise population.
**Footprint (Track)** **Four-toed, Stumpy** Tracks are clear, stumpy, and four-toed (resembling elephant tracks); unlike the flipper tracks of sea turtles.
**Feeding Trail** **Often Visible** Narrow trails of disturbed vegetation leading from the burrow apron to grazing areas; indicates an *active* burrow.
