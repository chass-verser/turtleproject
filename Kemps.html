Kemp's Ridley Sea Turtle (*Lepidochelys kempii*)

Slide 1: Species Overview & Conservation Status

Field Detail
**Scientific Name** *Lepidochelys kempii*
**Conservation Status** Federally Endangered (ESA); Critically Endangered (IUCN)
**Size (Adult)** 24–28 inches (60–70 cm) length; 70–100 lbs (32–45 kg) weight
**Distinguishing Feature** The smallest sea turtle species. The only species that regularly nests during the **daytime**.
**Diet** Carnivorous: Primarily blue crabs, mollusks, snails, shrimp, and occasional fish.

Slide 2: Habitat and Distribution

Primary Habitat (Gulf of Mexico)

  • **Adults:** Prefer shallow, nearshore coastal (neritic) habitats, usually with muddy or sandy bottoms. They rarely go deeper than 160 feet.
  • **Juveniles:** Spend 1–2 years in the open ocean (pelagic zone), often hiding and feeding in floating mats of **Sargassum algae**.

Location in Alabama

  • **Areas:** Mobile and Baldwin counties (along the Gulf Coast beaches).
  • **Status:** Regular, but infrequent, visitors and occasional nesters. Alabama is at the northern limit of their nesting range.
  • **Nesting:** A small number of nests are documented annually (e.g., 4 confirmed nests in 2023), making Alabama a vital area for monitoring.

Slide 3: Nesting Habits (Arribadas)

Characteristic Detail
**Nesting Season** May 1 – October 31 (Alabama); Primary nesting April – July.
**Primary Nesting Site** Rancho Nuevo, Tamaulipas, Mexico (Over 90% of global nesting).
**Unique Behavior** **Arribada** (Mass, synchronized, daytime nesting).
**Clutch Size** Average 100 eggs per nest.
**Incubation Period** Approx. 45–58 days (sex is determined by sand temperature).

Key Fact:

The "Arribada" is the synchronized, mass arrival of female turtles to the nesting beach, which occurs in the daytime, unlike most other sea turtle species.
